Empower your Career with the GRE Exam!
General Record Examination (GRE) - One of the world’s largest assessment programs for all the aspiring candidates looking to pursue masters' Studies and Ph.D. abroad. Created and administered by ETS (Educational Testing System), the GRE exam consists of the General Test and Subject Test. Generally, a candidate is required to attempt the GRE General Test, while Subject Tests are taken additionally to display expertise in that domain according to the target's school requirements. The GRE exam is available in three formats - Computer-based, Paper-based and Home-based to assess the candidates' analytical, verbal and quantitative skills.
Master the science behind the GRE Sections
Key Details

A maximum score of 340 can be achieved in the exam. The GRE General Test comprises 3 major sections
  1. Analytical Writing
  2. Verbal Reasoning
  3. Quantitative Reasoning
For the GRE Subject Test, the candidates are required to appear for any one of the following subjects according to the school’s requirement:
  1. Chemistry
  2. Mathematics
  3. Physics
  4. Psychology
Analytical Writing Assignment
Duration: 60 minutes.
In this section you will be given 2 tasks to complete in this section i.e. Analyzing an issue (30-minutes) and Analyzing an argument (30 minutes). This assignment measures your ability in critical thinking, to articulate and support complex ideas, to construct and evaluate arguments.
Verbal Reasoning
Duration: 60 minutes.
Verbal Reasoning consists of 2 sections (40 questions) with a time duration of 60 minutes.It measures your ability to analyze and evaluate written material in the form of sentences, recognize relationships among words and concepts, and analyze and identify the author's assumptions/perspective.
Quantitative Reasoning
Duration: 70 minutes.
Quantitative reasoning consists of 2 sections with a total of 40 questions to answer. This part of the exam measures your ability to interpret and analyze quantitative information, analyze data, and solve problems using mathematical techniques.
GRE comes with a restriction
The GRE Score is valid for 5 years and an individual can reattempt the GRE Exam in 21 days but only 5 times over a year. Apparently, there is no lifetime limit on the number of times an individual can attempt the GRE Exam.
Are you eligible to write the GRE Exam?
Know here.
There's no such eligibility set by ETS for taking the GRE Exam. There is no upper age limit for candidates. As this program is intended for applicants who are willing to take admissions to Post Graduate Programs, they are expected to possess a recognized graduate degree. The candidates are expected to check the eligibility criteria of the colleges/universities they are applying for, after taking the GRE Exam.
