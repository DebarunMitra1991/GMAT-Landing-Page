Graduate Management Aptitude Test (GMAT) - An entry ticket to reach your dream B-School and pursue MBA. GMAT Exam is a computer-based multiple-choice adaptive test for all aspiring graduates to pursue their MBA in top B-Schools globally. GMAT Exam is designed by GMAC (Graduate Management Admission Council) to assess the candidates' Mathematical Aptitude & English skills and preparedness in taking up their MBA Program.

The Structure of GMAT Exam

Analytical Writing Assignment

Duration: 30 minutes.

You'll be given a 30-minute writing assignment to analyze your writing skills and rated independently in two ways - a computerized evaluation and a person from GMAC evaluates and scores.

Integrated Reasoning

Duration: 30 minutes.

Integrated Reasoning is responsible for analyzing the reasoning abilities with the help of 12 questions of varied types like Multi-source reasoning questions, Graphics Interpretation Questions, Two-part Analysis Questions, and Table Analysis Questions.

Quantitative

Duration: 30 minutes.

The Quantitative section has 31 multiple choice questions with the types - Problem Solving and Data Sufficiency. Problem Solving Questions brings back your Arithmetic, Basic Algebra, and Elementary Geometry to life, whereas Data Sufficiency focusses on Quantitative Reasoning ability.

Verbal

Duration: 30 minutes.

Verbal section has 36 multiple choice questions of types - Sentence Correction, Critical Reasoning, and Reading Comprehension. The Verbal Section is solely responsible for analyzing your Language Proficiency in writing.

GMAT comes with a Limitation

You can take the GMAT Exam once in 16 days and up to 5 times in 365 days and a total of 8 times in a lifetime. Apparently, the GMAT Score is valid for 5 years.

Are you eligible to write the GMAT Exam?

Know here.

There's no such eligibility set by GMAC for taking the GMAT Exam. But, one should see to it that they're eligible for the colleges/universities they're applying for after taking the GMAT Test. The candidate must fall in between the age gap of 18 and maximum. Whereas the candidates between the ages of 13 and 17 should possess a letter from their parents or legal guardian.
